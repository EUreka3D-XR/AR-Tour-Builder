## 2. Collection and Processing of Personal Data 

### 2.1 Confidentiality and Security of Information 

The processing of personal data by the AR Tour Builder tool is conducted in a manner that ensures both confidentiality and security thereof. More specifically, it is carried out by authorised personnel, exclusively authorized for this purpose whereas all appropriate organizational and technical measures are taken to ensure data security and the protection thereof against accidental or unlawful destruction, accidental loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure or access, as well as and any other form of unfair processing. The AR Tour Builder tool does not, in any way, transfer/transmit or disclose the participants’ personal data to any third party business organizations, natural persons or legal entities, public authorities or agencies or any other organizations, other than those specifically referred to herein. Project’s participants’ personal data may be communicated or transferred to government authorities and/or law enforcement officers, if that is required for the above purposes, or within the scope of enforcing a court decision or order, or for complying with a provision of law, or if so required in order to serve the legitimate interests of the National Technical University of Athens as Data Controller, in accordance with applicable law. 

### 2.2 Types of data processed 

When the user visits the AR Tour Builder platform and as long as they interact with it, some information may be collected, such as: 

a) Information that enables the registration and identification of the user. Authentication is managed through EGI Check-in, which ensures that registration information (username and password) is processed lawfully, securely, and only for authorized scientific and operational purposes, granting users full rights to access, correct, or delete their data. 

b) Information that enables the provision of the platform’s services, including: 

1. An Email address that is not verified or used by the platform, but is needed to link the user account with the EGI Check-in.

2. A Username on the platform that can be chosen freely (uniquely).

3. Navigation data within the platform, such as data related to the tours created or managed by the user and uploaded files.

4. Group relationships to other users that are identified by their Email.

### 2.3 Purpose of processing the collected data 

The personal data collected by the platform serve the following purposes: 

1. The provision of the platform’s services to the users, i.e.

1) The identification of the platform’ users, such as during registration and login; 

2) Provision of certain services, such as the creation, edit and retrieval of projects, tours and associated assets;

3) Group relationship data enables the collaboration and sharing functionality;

4) Seamless operation of the platform; 

5) User-friendly and easy-to-use platform user interface and improved internet experience during the operation of the platform. 

B. The monitoring and debugging of the tool. Technical logs necessary for the operation, monitoring and security of the system are maintained. They are purged regularly but some entries can be linked back to user  identities.

## 3. Cookie Policy 

### 3.1 Service Application Cookies

When you access and/or use the Website, the tool places two types of Cookies on the user’s device. The tool sends two application-level cookies to the clients, a session id token and a CSRF token, used as a security mechanism to protect against Cross-Site Request Forgery. 

### 3.2 How long are cookies stored on the user’s device?

The duration for which a cookie will be stored on your browsing device depends on whether it is a "persistent" or a "session" cookie. The session cookies are stored on the user’s device until they close their browser tab. "Persistent cookies" (i.e. the CSRF cookie) remain on the user’s device after they have finished browsing until they expire or until the user deletes them.

### 3.3 How can the user disable cookies that have been placed on the their device?

You can usually use your web browser to enable, disable, or delete cookies. To do so, follow the instructions provided for by your web browser (usually located in the "Help", "Tools" or "Edit" settings). You can also set your web browser to reject all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent.
